# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Finance Planner is for individuals, couples, and small families who want a private, reliable place to manage income, spending, accounts, goals, budgets, subscriptions, and shared workspaces. Users are practical, recurring users: they come back weekly or monthly to enter transactions, check progress, and make decisions.

## Product Purpose

The product replaces ad hoc spreadsheets with a clean financial operating system. Success means a user can sign in, create a workspace, understand their financial month, and confidently maintain their data without feeling like they are doing accounting work.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a serious personal finance tool: quiet enough for repeated use, polished enough to feel like a real SaaS product, and direct enough that people do not need instructions for standard workflows.

## Anti-references

Avoid spreadsheet-like grids as the primary experience, decorative fintech clichés, navy-and-gold finance styling, neon crypto aesthetics, oversized marketing heroes inside the app, glassmorphism, gradient text, and card grids that repeat the same icon-heading-text pattern without purpose.

## Design Principles

- Make the next financial action obvious.
- Show useful numbers before asking users to configure everything.
- Prefer dense but readable operational surfaces over marketing composition.
- Use familiar SaaS patterns consistently: sidebar, header, forms, tables, filters, empty states.
- Treat privacy and workspace boundaries as product behavior, not implementation detail.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, visible focus states, clear form errors, and responsive layouts for desktop and mobile. Avoid relying on color alone for status, especially in budget and transaction states.
